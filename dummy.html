science What are Cells?

In biology, the cell is the smallest unit that can live on its own and that makes up all living organisms and the tissues of the body. A cell has three main parts: the cell membrane, the nucleus, and the cytoplasm.

Unicellular

Organisms that consist of a single cell, like bacteria or amoeba.

Multicellular

Organisms made of many cells, like plants, animals, and humans.

Cell Theory

The Cell Theory is one of the basic principles of biology. It was developed in the mid-1800s and states that:

  • check_circle All living organisms are composed of one or more cells.
  • check_circle The cell is the basic unit of structure and organization in organisms.
  • check_circle Cells arise from pre-existing cells.
